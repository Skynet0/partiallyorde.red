Letters of Recommendation

If you're reading this page, it's because you've asked me (or are thinking of asking me) to write you a letter of recommendation.

I'm happy to provide letters where they can be helpful, but I also want to be transparent about what kinds of letters I'm able to provide, and where I'm comfortable submitting them. Different applications have different expecations of letters. Writing a strong letter takes a significant amount of time and effort on my part, but not all letters need to be strong.

Short Letters

Some examples of programs that I am fine writing short letters for include:

  • SURFs, or other summer research programs
  • Study abroad
  • Coursework-based masters programs
  • The "third letter" for graduate programs with two other very strong letters

These sorts of letters tend to consist of "X took my course and earned [insert grade here]", potentially with some descriptions of our interactions. Again, these are not strong letters, but they may be sufficient for your application.

To request a letter from me, make sure to ask me in-person or over email first. Once I've agreed to write you a letter (and directed you to this page), send me the following information at least two weeks before your earliest deadline:

  • Your preferred name and pronouns
  • Your latest resume/CV
  • A copy of your (unofficial) transcript.
    • Please acknowledge that you waive your FERPA rights so that I can discuss your grade(s) in the letter.
  • A list of the programs that you're applying for, and for each:
    • The deadline for my letter
    • A short description of why you're applying for it (this could be a summary of your statement of purpose or similar)
  • At least 2 distinct interactions, moments, projects, or similar (aside from your final grade) that would make good things to talk about in the letter (e.g. class participation, conversations/discussions with me, going above-and-beyond on projects, etc.)

When you submit your letter requests, make sure to waive your right to view submitted letters.

Strong Letters

Some examples of applications that I will need to know you better for include:

  • Competitive research-based masters programs
  • PhD applications
  • Graduate fellowships, scholarships, and similar awards
  • Jobs in academia

The students that I can most effectively write a strong letter for include those who have been a teaching assistant or have done research with me in at least two distinct quarters. I may also be able to write these letters if we've had extensive interactions both in and outside of class, and you've gone above and beyond the course requirements; but they won't be as strong as for students with whom I have a strong working relationship.

If you're not sure what letter you need, or whether I can write you a strong letter, please ask.

To request this kind of letter from me, make sure to ask me in-person or over email first. Once I've agreed to write you a strong letter (and directed you to this page), send me the following information at least two weeks before your earliest deadline:

  • Everything in the list above for short letters
  • At least 3 more distinct interactions, moments, projects, or similar that would make good things to talk about in the letter. These should cover the different ways in which we've interacted (student, TA, research, etc.), and I should be able to write about a paragraph about each individual thing.
  • Draft of the materials you're submitting for the application (e.g. full statement of purpose/research, etc.)
